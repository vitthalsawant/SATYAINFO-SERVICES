Teeth Scaling

  1. Home
  2. Teeth Scaling

Maintaining good oral hygiene is crucial, but sometimes brushing and flossing alone aren’t enough. Teeth scaling, a professional dental procedure, is designed to give your teeth and gums a deeper level of care by removing harmful plaque and tartar buildup. It’s a preventive step that helps ensure your oral health stays on track.

What Is Teeth Scaling?

Teeth scaling is a non-surgical dental treatment that removes plaque and hardened tartar (calculus) from above and below the gumline. It is often performed alongside root planing, which smooths the tooth root to promote gum reattachment and prevent bacteria from accumulating.

Why Is Teeth Scaling Important?

Plaque and tartar buildup can lead to serious oral health issues if left untreated. Here’s why scaling is an essential part of dental care:

  • Prevents Gum Disease: Scaling removes bacteria and plaque that can cause gingivitis and periodontitis.
  • Protects Tooth Structure: By reducing tartar, scaling helps prevent cavities and decay.
  • Improves Overall Health: Healthy gums reduce the risk of systemic health issues like heart disease and diabetes.
  • Eliminates Bad Breath: Removing plaque and tartar helps keep your breath fresh and odor-free.

How Is Teeth Scaling Performed?

The procedure is straightforward and typically completed in a single visit, though multiple sessions may be needed for severe cases:

  1. Examination: The dentist examines your teeth and gums, identifying areas with plaque and tartar buildup.
  2. Scaling: Special ultrasonic tools and manual instruments are used to remove tartar above and below the gumline.
  3. Root Planing (if needed): The root surfaces are smoothed to help gums heal and reattach.

Post-Scaling Care Tips

To maximize the benefits of teeth scaling and maintain a healthy mouth, follow these simple tips:

  • Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush and fluoride toothpaste.
  • Floss regularly to remove food particles and plaque from between teeth.
  • Rinse with an antimicrobial mouthwash to reduce bacteria.
  • Stay consistent with regular dental visits for check-ups and cleanings.

"Teeth scaling is not just about cleaning your teeth; it’s about protecting your gums, your smile, and your overall health."

Is Teeth Scaling Right for You?

If you’re experiencing gum swelling, bleeding, or bad breath, teeth scaling might be the solution you need. Talk to your dentist to determine whether it’s the right treatment for your oral health. Invest in scaling today to keep your teeth and gums in top shape for years to come!
